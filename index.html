Cyclistic Bikeshare – Case Study

By Mesula Korsa • Portfolio Edition
Sections: Executive Summary • Data & Method • Findings • Recommendations • Ethics

Executive Summary

Why this matters. Annual members are more profitable than casual riders. The goal was to understand behaviour differences so Cyclistic can convert casuals to members and improve service where it counts.

What the data shows. Members account for ~64% of trips. Usage peaks in July–August and dips in January. Casual riders cluster on weekends (leisure), while members show a weekday commute pattern (notable Tuesday spike). Classic & electric bikes dominate; docked bikes are rarely used by members.

What to do. Focus conversion in Mar–Aug with a Summer‑Pass→Annual ladder, a Weekend‑Only pilot, and a Commute bundle that rewards weekday streaks. Operationally, increase e‑bike availability and tighten rebalancing around weekend/summer surges; reassess docked bikes in member‑dense areas.

Dataset: Jan–Dec 2023 monthly CSVs. Cleaning in Power Query; star schema with Trips fact + Calendar/Station dimensions; DAX for measures.

Data & Method

  • Data: 12 monthly CSVs (2023). Combined in Power Query; enforced types; dropped nulls; removed outliers where appropriate.
  • Modeling: Trips fact table + Calendar and Station dimensions; DAX measures for member share, seasonality, weekday patterns, and bike‑type mix.
  • Limits: No pricing, demographics, or weather data in the case study; results reflect usage patterns, not LTV or price sensitivity.

Recommendations

  • Marketing & pricing (Mar–Aug): Summer Pass→Annual; Weekend‑Only pilot; commute bundle rewarding AM/PM streaks.
  • Service & operations: Target rebalancing at Fri PM/Sat AM (leisure) and weekday peaks (commute). Raise e‑bike share before peak season.
  • Measurement: Primary KPI = casual→member conversion; Secondary = availability SLA, rides/member; North‑star = 12‑month retention/LTV.

Ethics & Notes

This public case uses the Cyclistic capstone dataset (no personal identifiers). For health projects, follow HIPAA de‑identification and FAIR data practices.
